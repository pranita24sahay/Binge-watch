Stranger Things

A love letter to the '80s classics that captivated a generation, Stranger Things is set in 1983 Indiana, where a young boy vanishes into thin air. As friends, family and local police search for answers, they are drawn into an extraordinary mystery involving top-secret government experiments, terrifying supernatural forces and one very strange little girl. ...
One of the most realistic depictions of the joyful growing pains that come from childhood and teenage years. A story about truly loveable characters, brilliant writing, and what happens when hearts burgeoning with curiosity, fear, and hope clash with a terrible force.

The Vampire diaries

Started as a feud between the brothers,this series is power packed with drama,action and romance. it starts with a suspense,when the Mystic Falls gets a mysterious but hot new guy in the college,who has all the girls swooning over him,but apparently he has eyes for only one. ...
It takes a turn of events when Ian somerhalder shows up,and both of them fight for the affection of Nina Dobrev(aka Elena Gilbert) and the life of a simple college girl takes a u-turN The most important thing to know about this show is the first four episodes are seemingly pointless; focused on cheap thrills, romance, and teen drama. Then something magical happens, creeping up silently and latching on without the viewer's notice: interest. By the end of the fourth episode the many story lines pick up and take off, and suddenly a silly or meaningless event becomes important. Things move fast in "The Vampire Diaries." This is a show that takes risks: people die without warning, stories that you thought could go on forever take only a few episodes, characters do and experience things you wouldn't have thought possible. Even better, there is no character who is wholly unlikeable. Everyone in the large ensemble cast has a story and a 3-dimensional structure, and even if the viewer decidedly doesn't like a character, the swing from one storyline to the next never lingers in one place for too long. I can almost safely guarantee anyone who likes vampires and horror (and a lot of dry wit) will not be disappointed.

You

This is a series that engulfs mystery and suspense at levels that will make you scratch out your hair. It makes you question what is the extent of loving someone ? Traditionally, the key to making a good serial killer TV series is to make the audience sympathize with the killer. He was a terrible killer, but you could feel empathy for him because he only killed bad guys. And the writers did a great job making sense of his “dark passenger”. ...
In the case of You, I think they intentionally avoided making Joe a sympathetic serial killer. The guy is a real perv with very questionable motives and indefensible ethics.But that's exactly what makes the character interesting and original. I didn’t get that this is where the writers were going until after the second murder. For that matter, his state of mind is probably closer to that of a real serial killer.
And just when you think ,you have figured out the entire plot of the story,you are thrown off-guard by a event,that makes you jump out of your chair saying "woah!"
